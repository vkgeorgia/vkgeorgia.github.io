My services are organized around the decisions leaders typically face, not around tools or frameworks. Each one is a structured way to turn a complex technology question into trade-offs the business can act on.

Three of them are usually triggered when something needs to be repaired or restructured (**FIX**). Three are usually triggered when something new is being built (**BUILD**). In most real engagements, the two sides overlap.

## Executive Architecture Advisory (FIX)

**Stabilizing IT landscapes under pressure and recovering stalled initiatives.**

For situations where a major programme is off-track or the IT landscape has lost coherence, and leadership needs an independent view of what is actually going on.

- Recover critical projects that are over budget, delayed, or losing sponsor confidence.
- Untangle vendor and integration dependencies that block change.
- Re-establish operational visibility and control at the leadership level.

→ [Learn more](/services/executive-architecture-advisory/)

## Enterprise Architecture Consulting (FIX)

**Aligning organizational structure, processes, and systems with the current strategy.**

Most often used during reorganization, M&A integration, or after a significant shift in business model.

- Map the real landscape of capabilities, systems, and costs — not the documented one.
- Identify overlap and duplication, and decide what to keep, consolidate, or retire.
- Design a lean operating model that fits the new organization.

→ [Learn more](/services/enterprise-architecture-consulting/)

## Technology Strategy Advisory (BUILD)

**Making technology choices the business can defend — on cost, risk, and long-term fit.**

Focused on high-stakes choices: build vs. buy, cloud vs. on-premise, platform vs. point solution, in-house vs. vendor.

- Test investment hypotheses against architectural reality.
- Compare options on cost, risk, speed, reversibility, and strategic fit.
- Define the criteria and KPIs against which the choice will be judged.

→ [Learn more](/services/technology-strategy-advisory/)

## Digital Transformation Advisory (BUILD)

**Moving transformation programmes from slides to shipping.**

For organizations whose transformation agenda is clear on strategy but losing momentum on execution.

- Break analysis paralysis with scoped prototypes and MVPs.
- Enforce sequence and scope discipline across distributed teams.
- Connect delivery outcomes back to the business case.

→ [Learn more](/services/digital-transformation-advisory/)

## Application Portfolio Optimization (FIX)

**Structurally reducing run-costs and legacy drag.**

Used when too much of the IT budget is consumed by "keeping the lights on", leaving little room for new capabilities.

- Audit the full application and license landscape.
- Identify systems that cost more than the value they generate.
- Produce and execute a sequenced decommissioning and consolidation plan.

→ [Learn more](/services/application-portfolio-optimization/)

## AI Readiness & Governance (BUILD)

**Architecting AI adoption as a managed capability, not a pilot factory.**

For organizations moving beyond isolated AI experiments toward integrated use in real processes.

- Design where and how AI is allowed to operate, and which data it can touch.
- Structure knowledge and integration layers so AI assistants and agents produce reliable results.
- Set up governance and evaluation so adoption can be defended to a board or regulator.

## How to engage

I work best with leaders who want to make a specific decision or resolve a specific situation, not with teams looking for additional hands.

- **Good fit**: you are about to reorganize, integrate, scale, restructure costs, or seriously adopt AI — and you want an architectural perspective before committing the budget or the team.
- **Poor fit**: staff augmentation, steady-state administration, or engagements where the real decision has already been made and only validation is wanted.

Engagement formats usually take one of three shapes:

- **Fixed-scope project — 6 to 16 weeks.** Assessment, target operating model, portfolio rationalization, architecture audit, or a specific transformation decision.
- **Fractional or embedded advisor — months.** Regular rhythm with CTO/CIO/CEO on a series of related decisions; typically a few days per week or per month.
- **Expert due diligence or second opinion — days to weeks.** Independent read on a portfolio company or a specific technology decision, for boards, investors, and PE firms.
